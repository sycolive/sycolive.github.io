Best Facebook Security Settings Guide

A comprehensive, step-by-step guide to secure every aspect of your Facebook account.

1. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) Setup

This is the most critical step. Enable 2FA using an authenticator app (like Google Authenticator or Duo) rather than SMS for maximum security.

  • Go to **Settings & Privacy > Settings > Security and Login.**
  • Select **"Use two-factor authentication."**
  • Choose **"Authentication App"** (recommended) or **"Text Message (SMS)."**
  • **IMPORTANT:** Backup the recovery codes in a safe place!

2. Security and Login Alerts

Ensure you get notified of any suspicious login attempts or unrecognized devices immediately.

  • Under **Security and Login**, set up alerts for unrecognized logins via **Email, Messenger, and Facebook notifications.**
  • Regularly check the **"Where You're Logged In"** section and log out of any devices you don't recognize.
  • Change your password if you see any unknown devices.

3. Friend Request and Tagging Review

Control who can interact with your profile and share your posts for better privacy.

  • Go to **Settings > Privacy.**
  • Set **"Who can send you friend requests?"** to **"Friends of friends."**
  • Enable **"Review posts you're tagged in before the post appears on your profile."**
  • Set **"Who can see your future posts?"** to **"Friends"** or **"Custom"** (not Public).

4. Limit Past Posts (Bulk Privacy)

Apply privacy restrictions to your old posts in bulk to hide them from the public.

  • Go to **Settings > Privacy.**
  • Use the option: **"Limit who can see posts you've shared with friends of friends or Public?"** to change them all to **"Friends"** privacy level.

5. Remove Unused Apps/Websites

Third-party apps often request excessive permissions and can be security risks. Remove access from apps you no longer use.

  • Go to **Settings > Apps and Websites.**
  • Remove any apps/games that you haven't used recently or don't fully trust. Choose the option to **"Delete posts, videos, or events these apps published on your timeline."**

6. Trusted Contacts

Set up trusted contacts who can help you recover your account if you lose access (e.g., forgotten password or hacking).

  • Go to **Settings > Security and Login > Setting up extra security.**
  • Select 3-5 close, trusted friends who can send you a special recovery code if needed.

By following these steps, you can achieve a "Pro-Level" of security for your Facebook account. If you need professional assistance to secure your account, please check our **Our Services** section or contact us directly.
